Government Responsibilities

Know Your Rights

Right to Information

The Right to Information (RTI) is a powerful tool designed to empower every citizen and hold the government accountable. It gives you the legal right to ask for and receive information held by any public authority, such as government departments, agencies, and public institutions. The government's duty under the RTI Act is threefold:

Transparency: To proactively publish key information about its decisions, budgets, and work so that you don't always have to ask for it.

Responsiveness: To designate Public Information Officers (PIOs) who are legally required to reply to your information requests in a timely manner, usually within 30 days.

Accountability: To ensure all government workings, finances, and project details are open to public review, thereby fighting corruption and inefficiency.

By using your right to information, you are not just curiousâ€”you are becoming an informed participant in democracy, ensuring public money is spent wisely and services are delivered correctly. The government works for you, and RTI is your right to check their work!

Right to Equality

The Right to Equality is a fundamental principle that ensures fairness and justice for every person in the country. It means that the government cannot treat people differently based on factors like their religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birthâ€”everyone is equal before the law. The government's core duty under this right is to abolish discrimination and ensure that all its policies, laws, and decisions apply equally to all citizens. This right also means that all citizens must have equal opportunity in public employment and that the government must actively work to remove social inequalities. By upholding this right, the government guarantees that no one is above the law and that every individual has the chance to thrive without unfair barriers.

Freedom of Speech

The Freedom of Speech and Expression is the lifeline of any true democracy, and the government has a fundamental duty to protect it. This right guarantees that every citizen can freely share their thoughts, opinions, and ideasâ€”whether through speaking, writing, art, or peaceful protestâ€”without fear of punishment from the state. The government's core responsibility is to create a climate where open discussion thrives and to ensure that people can criticize leaders and policies without being silenced.

However, this right is not absolute; the government also has the duty to put in reasonable limits to protect everyone else. This means they can step in only when speech directly threatens public order, national security, decency, or when it incites immediate violence or defamation against another person. The duty of the government is to protect your voice, while also ensuring that freedom is used responsibly and safely for the good of all.

Where Your Taxes Go

The government has a fundamental responsibility to ensure that tax money is managed openly, wisely, and for the benefit of all citizens. When you pay taxesâ€”whether income tax, sales tax, or property taxâ€”you have a right to know how that money is being spent. The government must be transparent about its budget, showing exactly how funds are divided among different public services. This includes funding vital areas like: education (building and maintaining schools), healthcare (running hospitals and public clinics), infrastructure (fixing roads and building bridges), national security (defense and law enforcement), and social welfare (supporting those in need). The duty of the government is to spend your taxes efficiently and honestly, avoid waste, and be fully accountable to you, the taxpayer, for every dollar spent.

The government has a major responsibility to actively fight and prevent corruption at all levels of public service. This duty begins with establishing and strictly enforcing strong anti-corruption laws that clearly define corrupt actsâ€”like bribery, fraud, and misuse of public fundsâ€”and ensure serious consequences for those who break them. The government must promote complete transparency in its operations, especially in how contracts are awarded, licenses are granted, and public money is spent, making this information easily accessible to citizens. Furthermore, it is essential to protect whistleblowersâ€”those who report corruptionâ€”from harm or unfair treatment, and to create independent agencies that can investigate and prosecute corrupt officials without political interference. By building a culture of honesty and accountability, the government fulfills its duty to ensure public resources are used for the common good, not private gain.

Your Voice Matters

The government has a vital duty to ensure that the voices and concerns of all citizens are heard and seriously considered in the democratic process. This means creating and maintaining accessible platforms for public participation that go beyond just voting, such as holding open town hall meetings, providing easy ways to contact representatives, and allowing for public comment on new laws and policies. The government must also actively work to protect the right to peaceful assembly and protest, recognizing that public dissent is a healthy and necessary part of democracy. Most importantly, leaders have a responsibility to not just hear opinions but to listen actively to diverse communities, especially those who are often marginalized, and to let these voices influence their decisions. By welcoming feedback and debate, the government ensures that its actions truly reflect the needs and will of the people it serves.
