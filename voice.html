Raise Your Voice

Steps for citizens to document issues, file complaints, and use RTI.

File Complaint

Official portals to register issues and seek redressal:

CPGRAMS (Centralized Public Grievance Redress and Monitoring System):

Lodge grievances with any Central or State Government department.

Link:

CPGRAMS Officicial Website

National Consumer Helpline (NCH):

Register complaints regarding unfair trade practices, goods, and services (under the Department of Consumer Affairs).

Link:

NCH Officicial Website

SCORES (SEBI Complaint Redress System):

Lodge investor complaints against entities regulated by the Securities and Exchange Board of India.

Link:

SCORES Officicial Website

Bima Bharosa Portal (IRDAI):

Register grievances related to insurance claims and policies (managed by the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India).

Link:

IRDAI Officicial Website

RTI Application

Steps for Filing RTI Online (Central Government)


Identify the Authority:

Determine the exact Central Ministry/Department that holds the information you need (e.g., Ministry of Finance, UIDAI, EPFO).

Go to the Official Portal:

Visit the official Central Government RTI portal: https://rtionline.gov.in/

Start Request:

Click on the "Submit Request" option. You will be prompted to read and agree to the guidelines.

Fill the Form:

Select the correct Ministry/Department from the dropdown menu.

Enter Personal Details:

Fill in your personal information (Name, Address, Mobile Number, Email ID).

Write the Application:

In the "Text for RTI Request application" box, clearly and simply state the exact information you are seeking. Be specific and keep the text concise (usually limited to 3000 characters).

BPL Status:

Declare if you are Below Poverty Line (BPL). If yes, upload a copy of your BPL certificate to be exempt from the fee.

Pay the Fee:

If you are not BPL, click "Make Payment." The fee is usually â‚¹10 and can be paid online using Net Banking, Debit Card, or Credit Card.

Submit and Track:

After successful payment, a unique Registration Number will be generated. Save this number to track the status of your application on the portal using the "View Status" option.

Note:

The concerned department is legally required to respond within 30 days. If you do not receive a satisfactory reply, you can file a First Appeal through the same portal.
